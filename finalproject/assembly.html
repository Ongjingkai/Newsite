How to assemble

I design this so simple that a kid is able to figure out(It's tested on 13 year old kids). In case you don't, follow the model below.


Any other modification are to be made by a teen or adult, please do this under adult supervison if you are unsure how to use tools.

Front Assembly

Ensure the area the design would slide into is clear of any glue as the area MUST be clear to allow the designs to be changed easily


Back Assembly

Drill holes(5mm) for the LEDs on the back take note that the leds are arrange to fit the 2 designs I have. Please use a LED stips if you like to use more than 3 designs.


This area is where all the electronics are kept. As this area cannot be seen, Fitting of the electronics can be done without any concern with great care and proper management. This is a good time to glue your leds in as well.

Electronics Cover

Drill(or lasercut) a 10mm hole to allow the IR sensor to be installed.


Because I was lazy, I manage to damage the piece when drilling. Please learn from my mistake and place something in between the the clamp and the clamping surface 乁(ಥ ͜ʖಥ)ㄏ

Waxing

Wax the front surface to reduce the friction between sliding parts.


If you want to know why waxing wood would allow the parts to move better click here.

Electronics fitting

Firstly, install the switch, plug and light indicater(This 3 item is something required by the teachers for this project, you can choose to ignore this process if you are building it on your own)


By using heatstrink after soldering, you would prevent any short circuits or damage in the long run.



This is the first test using arduino nano, I would start with testing out using 5v before moving the the 12v used in thinkercad.



Using a power supply, I am able to find out the voltage and current needed to power everything.



It's at this point I relise that the wires would be a pain and wire management would be needed.



I reduced the wire down into the 2 mini breadboard base on my circuit.



This would reduce the space taken up by wire, this would help with future maintainence and learning.
